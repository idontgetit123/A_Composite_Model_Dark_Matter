# Speculation Document: Dark Composite Structures and Their Possible Origins

## Overview

This document explores a speculative framework in which dark matter is composed of geometric–capacitive triplet structures built from three functional roles:

- **u-role**: positive contributor  
- **d-role**: neutral/dielectric stabilizer  
- **e-role**: negative contributor  

These roles are not literal Standard Model quarks or electrons, but functional analogs that map onto the O(o)O geometric-capacitance module. The goal is to classify all possible triplet combinations, explore their potential stability, and consider how such composites might form in extreme environments such as black hole horizons.

## 1. Standard Model Context (What We Know)

In known physics, quarks combine into stable three-body structures called baryons:

- **Proton**: `uud`  
- **Neutron**: `udd`

These demonstrate that nature already favors **triplet confinement**, where internal roles (positive, neutral, negative contributions to field structure) determine the composite’s behavior.

This motivates the idea that **dark composites** could also be triplets, but bound not by the strong force, but by **geometric capacitance, resonance, and figure‑8 confinement** as described in the O(o)O module.

## 2. Proposed Dark Composites (ude, dde, edd, etc.)

A central candidate is the **neutraltron**, a balanced triplet composed of:

- one positive contributor (u-role)  
- one neutral/dielectric stabilizer (d-role)  
- one negative contributor (e-role)  

This yields the **ude family**, which is neutral externally and stable internally due to resonance locking. Variants such as **dde** or **edd** represent negative‑biased composites, potentially acting as “dark electron analogs” or negatons.

These structures may form families analogous to baryons, but governed by geometric resonance rather than QCD.

## 3. Black Hole Processing as a Source of Dark Composites

Black holes provide an extreme environment where normal matter is stripped to its fundamental charge and field components. Near the event horizon:

- curvature is extreme  
- charge separation is intense  
- quantum fluctuations are amplified  
- Hawking radiation re-emits field excitations  

If the vacuum supports geometric-capacitance modes, then a positive contributor, a negative contributor, and a dielectric mode may lock into a stable resonance pattern. This could produce:

- **neutraltrons** (balanced ude-type composites)  
- **negatons** (dde/edd-type composites)  
- **other dark triplets** depending on local field bias  

In this view, black holes act not only as sinks for ordinary matter but as **factories** that convert visible matter into dark modular composites.
